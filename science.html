Scientific Validation

The Clinical Science Baseline

While raw fermentations have sustained human populations for thousands of years, modern medicine highlights exactly why these metabolic structures are vital. Peer-reviewed studies verify that Lactic Acid Bacteria (LAB) act as immunomodulators, strengthening mucosal cell junctions.

By converting complex dietary fibers into short-chain fatty acids (like acetate, propionate, and butyrate), these live cultures lower local colon pH, forming a barrier against pathogen invasions.

At CULTURA, we cross-reference every batch with molecular strain markers to guarantee cellular survivability in stomach acids.

Microbial Genomics

Our Core Probiotic Strains

Metagenomic profiles of the dominant bacteria found in CULTURA kimchi and milk kefirs.

Lactobacillus plantarum (CL-02)

Highly resilient strain found in cabbage ferments. Known for its ability to survive gastric bile acids, helping synthesize L-lysine and promote bloating relief.

Leuconostoc mesenteroides (CL-05)

Initiates the early stages of classic lactic fermentation, producing high volumes of metabolic carbon dioxide which prevents ambient yeast mold.

Lactobacillus rhamnosus (KE-12)

Prevalent in our milk kefirs. Widely studied for supporting immune barrier cell tight-junctions and regulating vaginal/gut ecosystem ratios.

Saccharomyces kefir (KE-08)

A beneficial probiotic yeast grain cofactor that digests remaining lactose sugars, converting them into healthy enzymes and supporting gut motility.
